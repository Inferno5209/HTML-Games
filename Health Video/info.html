Abstinence

A comprehensive look at abstinence as a contraceptive method — what it is, how it works, and why it matters.

📖

What Is Abstinence?

Abstinence means choosing not to have sexual intercourse. It is a personal decision, not a device or medication. Unlike other contraceptive methods, abstinence does not involve anything physical you can see, use, or purchase. It works by completely avoiding sexual activity, preventing sperm from ever coming into contact with an egg.

⚙️

How It Works

Abstinence works through one straightforward principle: if no sexual intercourse occurs, pregnancy cannot happen. There is no chance for sperm to reach and fertilize an egg. When practiced consistently, it is the only method that is 100% effective at preventing both pregnancy and sexually transmitted infections (STIs).

Advantages & Disadvantages

✓ Advantages
  • 100% effective at preventing pregnancy
  • Fully prevents sexually transmitted infections
  • Completely free — no costs at all
  • No side effects (hormonal or physical)
  • Requires no doctor visits or prescriptions
  • Can be practiced by anyone at any time
  • No age restrictions or eligibility requirements
  • Reversible at any time — simply a personal choice
✗ Disadvantages
  • Requires strong self-control and willpower
  • Needs clear communication and agreement between partners
  • May be difficult to maintain long-term
  • Social or peer pressure can make it challenging
  • Does not address or treat any existing STIs
  • May not align with everyone's personal values or lifestyle
