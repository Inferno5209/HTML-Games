The Women's Suffrage Movement and the 19th Amendment

Follow the journey of women's fight for the right to vote through key moments in history.

About the Movement

The women's suffrage movement was a decades-long fight to win the right to vote for women in the United States. It took activists and reformers nearly 100 years to win that right, and the campaign was not easy.

1
1848

The Seneca Falls Convention marks the beginning of the women's suffrage movement. Elizabeth Cady Stanton presents the Declaration of Sentiments.

2
1869

Susan B. Anthony and Elizabeth Cady Stanton form the National Woman Suffrage Association to fight for a constitutional amendment.

3
1890

The National American Woman Suffrage Association (NAWSA) is formed, uniting suffrage groups across the nation.

4
1913

Over 5,000 women march in Washington D.C. for voting rights on the eve of President Wilson's inauguration.

5
1917

The "Silent Sentinels" begin picketing the White House, demanding action from President Wilson. Many are arrested and imprisoned.

6
1919

Congress passes the 19th Amendment on June 4th, sending it to the states for ratification. 36 states needed for approval.

7
1920

August 18, 1920: Tennessee becomes the 36th and final state needed to ratify the amendment by just one vote.

8
1920

August 26, 1920: The 19th Amendment is officially adopted. Women across America finally win the right to vote!
